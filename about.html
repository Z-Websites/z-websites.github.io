Our Purpose

Build a coordinated engine for musculoskeletal innovation.

Indiana has deep clinical, research, manufacturing, workforce, and industry strengths. NSF IMPACT Engine brings those assets into a coordinated ecosystem so discoveries, technologies, talent, and partnerships can move more directly toward real-world use.

The Engine focuses on reducing fragmentation across sectors, connecting regional capabilities, and creating clearer pathways for partners who want to solve pressing musculoskeletal health challenges.

Our Region

Indiana is built for musculoskeletal innovation.

The Indiana MSK Innovation Triangle brings together academic, clinical, manufacturing, workforce, life sciences, and investment strengths that make the state a practical home for musculoskeletal health innovation.

  • Academic institutions with expertise in musculoskeletal health and engineering, including Indiana University, Purdue University, University of Notre Dame, Grace College, Ivy Tech Community College, Marian University, and Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology.
  • Research centers such as the Regenstrief Institute, the Indiana Center for Musculoskeletal Health, and IU Business Sustainability and Innovation Lab.
  • Major pharmaceutical, orthopedic, and device manufacturers, including Eli Lilly and Zimmer Biomet.
  • The Orthopedic Capital of the World in Warsaw, Indiana.
  • Heartland BioWorks, designated a Regional Technology and Innovation Hub by the U.S. Economic Development Administration.
  • A life sciences economy that made Indiana the largest exporter of life sciences products in the United States in 2024.
  • A growing venture network, with a record-breaking $1.05 billion in venture activity in 2024.
  • A global sports industry estimated to expand to $100 billion by 2030.

Melissa Kacena’s career spans biomedical engineering, translational bone research, and investigations into the relationship between bone and hematopoietic biology, with work in fracture repair that has led to patented innovations in orthopaedics. That perspective and record of innovation led to her role as one of the principal investigators on the NSF Engines proposal that established IMPACT.
